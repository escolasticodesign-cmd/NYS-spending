The Big Picture
New York spends more than it takes in — and the gap is growing every year.
🚨
New York Is Running a Deficit
The state brings in $248.9 billion but spends $254 billion — a gap of roughly $5.1 billion this year alone. Over the next 3 years, the projected shortfall grows to $34.3 billion. Spending is growing faster than revenue, and federal funding cuts could make this significantly worse.
⚠️
Federal Money Is a Big Risk
Nearly 39 cents of every dollar the state spends comes from the federal government — that's $96.7 billion. The vast majority (87%) funds health and social programs. If federal funding is cut, New York faces a major crisis with no easy fix.
✅
Strong Tax Collection
The state collected $162 billion in state and local taxes in FY2025. Personal income taxes — especially from high earners in finance — are the backbone of New York's revenue. Wall Street performance directly affects how much money the state has.

⚠️
More Than 3 Million May Exceed Income Limits
The Empire Center for Public Policy — a watchdog group — found that over 3 million enrollees appear to have incomes above the eligibility limits for Medicaid or the Essential Plan, based on census data analysis. For a family of four, New York now offers free coverage up to $78,000/year in household income — very close to the state's median family income.
📈
New York Is 7 Points Above Any Other State
At 44% enrollment, New York leads all 50 states by a wide margin — the next highest state is 7 percentage points lower, and the national average is 24%. For every 1 person removed from the "uninsured" count, 3+ people were added to Medicaid or the Essential Plan over the past decade.
Waste, Inefficiency & Red Flags
What government auditors, watchdogs, and independent analysts have flagged as wasteful or concerning.
🎬 Film Subsidies
$9.5B
Hollywood Tax Credits (2024–2036)
NYS will pay film and TV producers $9.5 billion over this period. Watchdog group Reinvent Albany says it costs $75,000 per job per year — one of the least efficient job-creation programs in the state.
🏢 Corp Subsidies
$4.5B/yr
Corporate Economic Development Spending
Reinvent Albany calls $4.5 billion/year in corporate subsidies "totally flawed, irrational, and wasteful trickle-down economics." Many of these programs have never been independently evaluated for effectiveness.
💸 No Oversight
$14.8B
"Lump Sum" Discretionary Spending
Billions are budgeted as vague "lump sums" — meaning the Governor and Legislature decide how to spend it later, often without public scrutiny or competitive bidding. The Comptroller has repeatedly warned this invites waste and corruption.
🏥 Medicaid
3M+
Potential Medicaid Over-Enrollment
Watchdog analysis suggests over 3 million enrollees may have incomes above the official eligibility limits. NY extended free coverage to households earning up to 250% of the federal poverty level — $78K/year for a family of four.
🎭 Broadway
$800M
Proposed Broadway Subsidies
Budget watchdogs urged lawmakers to reject $800 million in new Broadway industry subsidies, arguing that an already-profitable entertainment industry shouldn't need taxpayer-funded support of this scale.
📊 Structural
$34.3B
Growing Structural Budget Gap
The Citizens Budget Commission warned that the $254B budget "spends too much and does too little to prepare for federal cuts or a downturn." The 3-year gap jumped $7B in just 3 months — a sign of unsustainable spending growth.

🔎
Where to Dig Deeper
The State Comptroller publishes an annual audit report — the most recent (2024-25) found multiple agencies failing to properly oversee spending, track contracts, or prevent overpayments. In FY 2024-25, cyberattack losses in NYS exceeded $775 million. The Civil Enforcement Division collected over $2 billion in unpaid taxes and child support — meaning billions go uncollected each year.
